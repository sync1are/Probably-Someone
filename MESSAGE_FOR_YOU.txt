Here is what happened:
When you asked me to remove the Local Planner, we forced NVIDIA NIM to handle every single step of the tool loop. When ARIA clicks, types, and takes screenshots, it makes 3 or 4 rapid-fire API calls in under 10 seconds. 

Free-tier cloud APIs (like NVIDIA NIM) have strict rate limits (usually around 5 requests per minute). Because ARIA is moving so fast, NVIDIA blocked the connection and threw the `429 Too Many Requests` error you saw in your terminal.

This is exactly why I built the Local Planner! By using LM Studio to handle the rapid-fire clicking/screenshot steps (which is free and unlimited), we completely avoid NVIDIA's rate limit, and only use NVIDIA for the final summary.

I have just re-added the Local Planner back into `app.py`.
